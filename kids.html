Amazing Deep Sky Objects

Gamma Cygni Nebula (IC 1318)

This is a big cloudy nebula full of glowing gas. Hot stars inside make it shine with pretty colors. It is about 5,000 light-years away from Earth. That is very far!

IC 727 (Spiral Galaxy)

IC 727 is a spiral galaxy with arms that twist around. It has many stars and maybe new ones being born. It is about 435 million light-years away. That is super far away!

Pinwheel Galaxy (Messier 101)

The Pinwheel Galaxy looks like a spinning pinwheel toy. It has bright spiral arms with pink spots where new stars are born. It is about 21 million light-years away from us.

Messier 106

Messier 106 is a big spiral galaxy with bright arms. It has a very active center with a black hole. It is about 24 million light-years away from Earth.

Great Hercules Cluster (Messier 13)

The Great Hercules Cluster is a big ball of hundreds of thousands of old stars. There is also a far-away galaxy in the picture. The cluster is about 22,000 light-years away.

Whirlpool Galaxy (Messier 51)

The Whirlpool Galaxy looks like a swirling whirlpool in water. It has pretty spiral arms and is pulling in a smaller galaxy. It is about 25 million light-years away.

Ring Nebula (Messier 57)

The Ring Nebula looks like a shiny ring or donut in space. It is gas from an old star. There is also a tiny far galaxy nearby. The nebula is about 2,600 light-years away.

Sunflower Galaxy (Messier 63)

The Sunflower Galaxy looks like a big sunflower with fluffy petals. It has lots of spiral arms full of stars. It is about 37 million light-years away from Earth.

NGC 6339 (Barred Spiral Galaxy)

NGC 6339 is a spiral galaxy with a bar in the middle. It has open arms with stars and gas. It is about 94 million light-years away.

Crescent Nebula (NGC 6888)

The Crescent Nebula looks like a crescent moon made of glowing gas. A hot star blew the gas into this shape. It is about 5,000 light-years away.

NGC 7331 (Spiral Galaxy)

NGC 7331 is a big spiral galaxy seen a little from the side. It has a bright center and dusty lanes. It is about 40 million light-years away from us.

Bubble Nebula (NGC 7635)

The Bubble Nebula looks like a giant bubble in space. A strong wind from a bright star made the bubble shape. It is about 8,000 light-years away.

NGC 891 (Edge-on Spiral Galaxy)

NGC 891 is a spiral galaxy we see from the side. It has a thin line of dark dust across it. It is about 30 million light-years away.

NGC 925 (Spiral Galaxy)

NGC 925 is a spiral galaxy with loose, floppy arms. It has bright spots where stars are forming. It is about 30 million light-years away.

Tulip Nebula (Sh2-101)

The Tulip Nebula looks like a pretty tulip flower made of glowing gas. Hot stars inside make it shine red and blue. It is about 6,000 light-years away.
